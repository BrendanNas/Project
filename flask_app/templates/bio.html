Birth of the Band.

Way back in 1964, a man by the name of Guy Patterson, who aspired to become a jazz drummer, was asked by Jimmy Mattingly II and Leonard Haise to perform at a talent show with their band. After they managed to win the talent show, the three young men, along with their newest bass player T.B. Player, decided to tour around the world to spread their music. And that folks, is the birth of the the band known as The Oneders (not O'Neaters).

Meet the members.

Guy "Skitch" Patterson is the Oneders drummer, and a damn good one at that. He was born in 1947 in Virginia but eventually made his way up to Erie Pennsylvania where he spent his days working at an appliance store. One day, two young men named James and Leonard came into the store looking for him to ask for his help. They had heard he was a drummer, and they needed a drummer to help them in an upcoming talent show. Choosing that anything would be better then just staying in the store, he joined them and actually helped them win the talent show. After the show, Guy decided to stay with the band and went with them as they travelled around the country to perform at a variety of places.

James "Jimmy" Mattigly II is the Oneders lead singer as well as their rhythm guitarist. Born in 1945 in Texas to two musicians, he quickly discovered a love for music in all its forms. During his years at school, he met a young boy named Leonard and the two became friends almost instantaneously due to their shared love of music. The two would spend time practicing music in Leonards garage where he would sing the songs that they wrote. In 1960, the two took part in a festival in their home town where James sang all the songs that the two wrote, winning the hearts of the crowd with his phenomenal voice. Ever since, he's been the voice of The Oneders, and he will be for as long as he can.

Leondard "Lenny" Haise is the Oneders lead guitarist as well as one of the original founders of the band. He was born in 1948 in Arizona but moved Texas at the age of ten after his parents divorce. It was there that he met James and soon after the two became close friends while attending the same elementary school. In their early days, they would spend their free time practicing music in Leondard's garage (much to his fathers dismay) where Leonard would play the guitar while James was the vocalist. In 1960, the two decided to take part in a event held in their home town where Leonards father encouraged them to provide entertainment for the festival, leading the boys to spend the whole event playing music for the slowly congregating crowd. Soon, they decided to make their music official and created the band known as The Oneders (which was and is still misread as the O'Neaders).

T.B. "Gramps" Player serves as the bass player for The Oneders. Born in 1937 in Michigan into a poor family, Player didn't have many luxuries in his youth, sometimes leading to nights where they didn't have lights,heating, or even food. Once he managed to save up enough money, he went and bought himself a bass guitar and soon found he have a gift for the guitar. After moving to Pennsylvania, he went around doing freelance work for people who had a job to give, and money to give for a job, it wasn't the most thrilling life, but he enjoyed it. One way or another, he heard about the band known as The Oneders and decided to give them a shot. He soon managed to join the band and now helps to set up tours across the country for their band, and was nicknamed "Gramps" due to him being older then all the other band members.
